Our story

Cloverville is a small eco village where 42 residents work together to live more sustainably. We grow food, share tools and reduce waste wherever we can. To make our everyday green efforts visible, Green Bob created a simple system that tracks our sustainable actions, communal tasks and small point based trades. The website shares our ideas and shows how small actions add up to real community impact.

Green activities in Cloverville

Cycling instead of driving

50 points · weekly action

Cloverville residents often choose the bicycle instead of a car and record it as a green action. Cycling to work for a whole week is one of the most common examples. It is registered anonymously in the system, adds green points to the shared climate capital and appears in the catalogue of ideas so others can see what is possible without it turning into a competition.

Vegan dinner week

30 points · food action

Another typical green action in Cloverville is eating vegan for a week, often using vegetables grown in the village. Leftovers and peels are composted and later returned to the shared gardens as nutrient rich soil. Like all green actions, this is registered with green points and shown anonymously to inspire others to try similar changes in their everyday life.
